Career

Iggy Azalea started her career as a rapper at the age of 14 years. Initially, she also worked as a cleaner and worked in hotels and holiday houses. After that, she moved to America and worked there illegally but later she was approved as a permanent United States resident.

In the year 2011, she released her first project, a mixtape titled Ignorant Art. Her song Pussy gained huge popularity and fan following for her. After that, she released her first video song titled My World, and later in the same year, she released her debut studio album titled The New Classic.

In the year 2012, she released her first extended play titled Glory, and later in the same year, she released another extended play titled Millionaire Misfits. Since then Iggy has released numerous singles, extended plays, and albums in her career. She has also traveled across the world for various music tours like The New Classic Tour in the year 2014.

She has also been credited as a director for some of her popular music videos like Priscilla, Queen of the Desert, Showgirls, Clueless, and many other popular music videos.

Awards

Iggy Azalea has been nominated and won many popular awards throughout her career in the music industry. She has won the American Music Awards twice in her career in the Rap and Hip Hop categories. She has also won the MTV Video Music Award in the year 2014.

Apart from these, she has been nominated for the 57th Annual Grammy Awards in four different categories. She also received 12 nominations for the 2015 Billboard Music Awards and 5 nominations for the 2015 iHeartRadio Music Awards. In the year she has also been rewarded with the Woman of the Year award by GQ Australia.
